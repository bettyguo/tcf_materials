## 1. The exam (TCF Canada, current format as of 2026)

Administered by France Éducation International (FEI). Four mandatory sections, sat the same day, total ≈ 2 h 47 min.

| Section | Format | Items | Time | Score range |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Compréhension orale (CO) | Audio + MCQ (4 options, 1 correct) | 39 | 35 min | 100–699 |
| Compréhension écrite (CE) | Text + MCQ | 39 | 60 min | 100–699 |
| Expression écrite (EE) | 3 tasks, handwritten or computer-based | 3 | 60 min | 0–20 |
| Expression orale (EO) | Face-to-face with examiner, 3 tasks | 3 | 12 min (2 prep) | 0–20 |

**Question-weight distribution in CO (and parallel structure in CE):**

| CEFR | Items | Pts each | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| A1 | 4 | 3 | 12 |
| A2 | 6 | 9 | 54 |
| B1 | 9 | 15 | 135 |
| B2 | 10 | 21 | 210 |
| C1 | 6 | 26 | 156 |
| C2 | 4 | 33 | 132 |
| **Total** | **39** | — | **699** |

→ **B1 + B2 items = 49% of the score.** This is where the learner's time should disproportionately go. Mastering B2 alone (perfect score on those 10 items) = 210 pts ≈ NCLC 5 floor; combined with strong B1 = NCLC 7 threshold (453+/458+). Adding the 6 C1 items moves the learner into NCLC 8/9 territory.

**EE tasks:**
1. **Tâche 1** (B1 anchor): write a message (~60–120 words) reacting to a situation (asking, suggesting, inviting, complaining).
2. **Tâche 2** (B2 anchor): write an article / report / formal letter (~120–150 words) presenting facts or recounting an experience.
3. **Tâche 3** (C1 anchor): write an argumentative essay (~150–180 words) defending a position with reasoning.

Scored on 4 criteria each, on a scale yielding 0–20 overall. Rubric (from FEI grilles):
- Capacity to fulfil the communicative task
- Coherence and cohesion (connectors, paragraph structure)
- Lexical range and accuracy
- Morphosyntactic range and accuracy

**EO tasks:**
1. **Tâche 1** (B1 anchor, ≈1.5 min): obtain information from the examiner via questions.
2. **Tâche 2** (B2 anchor, ≈3 min): describe an experience / talk about a topic.
3. **Tâche 3** (C1 anchor, ≈5 min after 2 min prep): defend an opinion / argue a position based on a short document.

Scored on 4 criteria yielding 0–20:
- Communicative effectiveness
- Lexical range
- Morphosyntactic control
- Phonological control (pronunciation, intonation, fluency)

Per IRCC + FEI tables published 2024, in effect for 2026 testing:

### EE / EO

| Score /20 | CEFR | NCLC |
|---|---|---|
| 0–3 | A1 | <4 |
| 4–5 | A1/A2 | 4 |
| 6–9 | A2/B1 | 5 |
| 10–11 | B1 | 6 |
| 12–13 | B2 | 7 |
| 14–15 | B2 | 7 |
| 16–17 | C1 | 8/9 |
| 18–20 | C1/C2 | 9/10+ |

**Learner target operationalised:**
- **Minimum viable** (NCLC 7 all four): CO ≥ 458, CE ≥ 453, EE ≥ 12, EO ≥ 12. Unlocks +50 CRS bonus on top of English NCLC ≥ 9.
- **Stretch** (NCLC 8/9): CO ≥ 503/523, CE ≥ 499/524, EE/EO ≥ 14/16.
- **Ceiling** (NCLC 10+): CO/CE ≥ 549, EE/EO ≥ 18.

The corpus must, at minimum, reliably produce the **stretch** band.

## 3. The learner

- **Current level**: CEFR B1 (self-reported). Treat as B1 floor; first-week diagnostic will confirm.
- **Native cognitive context**: PhD-level (final-year AI researcher). Strong meta-learning skills; can absorb explicit grammar rules and tolerate dense notation. Do not infantilise content with "Bonjour les amis 🎉" energy. Use linguistic terminology freely (mode, aspect, valence, anaphora, hypotaxis).
- **Location**: Montréal. Has organic Quebec-French exposure; should leverage daily life (radio, métro signage, conversations) as natural immersion.
- **Time budget**: ~2 h/day × 7 days × 12 weeks ≈ 168 hours. Add ~30 h passive (commute audio, ambient radio) → ~200 h total.
- **Constraint**: no human tutor assumed in the base plan; the corpus must be self-sufficient with optional tutor integration points marked.

### Why 3 months from B1 to C1 is hard but not impossible

Conventional estimate: B1→B2 ≈ 200 h focused study; B2→C1 ≈ 200 h more. Total ≈ 400 h for B1→C1. The learner has ~200 h. Therefore:
- **Trajectory must be exam-shaped, not breadth-shaped.** The corpus does not teach "all of French C1." It teaches the slice of French that the TCF tests, with calibrated practice on TCF-style items.
- **B2 must be operationally complete by week 8.** Weeks 9–12 are C1 polish.
- **Daily output is non-negotiable.** Passive consumption alone caps at ~B2.

## 4. Non-goals

This corpus is explicitly NOT:
- A general "learn French" curriculum.
- A Quebec-French immersion course (Quebec lexicon is covered for *recognition*, not *production*).
- A textbook in the Latitudes / Alter Ego / Édito tradition (those exist; we cite them, we do not replace them).
- A chatbot or interactive tutor (those exist commercially; we ship static, auditable, offline-usable content).
- A replacement for TEF Canada prep (different exam, different scoring; out of scope).

## 6. Definition of done (project-level)

The project ships when:
1. All 8 phases pass their `PHASE_N_EVAL.md` with 0 blockers.
2. `pytest` passes for all build pipelines.
3. CI builds: MkDocs site, Pandoc PDF book, Anki `.apkg`, audio set — all without errors.
4. A learner can clone the repo, follow `README.md`, and within 30 minutes have: (a) a running local site, (b) a downloadable PDF, (c) an Anki deck installed, (d) a 12-week study plan opened to Day 1.
5. The audit register `AUDIT.md` shows < 5 unresolved `low`-confidence items per 1000 generated lines of French.
6. A random sample of 50 MCQ items reviewed by you (acting as final adversarial reviewer) finds: 0 ambiguous correct answers, 0 wrong-key items, ≤ 2 stylistically suboptimal distractors. If higher, fix and re-sample.
